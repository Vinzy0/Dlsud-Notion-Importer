# UDScraper — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
DLSUD students who want to quickly grab their assignments and files from the LMS without digging through it manually. They may or may not use Notion. They open the extension when they need to know what's due — it's a utility, not a dashboard they live in.

### Core Job To Be Done
Open → Scan → Get tasks. That's the whole flow. File scraping (PDFs, slides from whole modules/subjects) is the second headline feature.

### Tool Philosophy
This is a **scraper with optional Notion integration**, not a Notion tool. Notion is a bonus, not a requirement. The UI should never make Notion feel like a prerequisite to getting value.

### Clarity Goal
At first glance, a new user should immediately understand:
1. This scrapes their LMS assignments
2. It can also pull PDFs and files from whole modules

The primary action (scan) should be the most obvious thing on screen.

### Design Principles
1. **Action-first**: The scan button is the hero. Everything else is secondary.
2. **Zero-learning-curve**: A student who has never used the tool should know what to do without reading anything.
3. **No Notion gatekeeping**: Notion sync is a power feature, not the main event. Don't let it dominate the UI.
4. **Signal over noise**: Show what matters (tasks due, files found) — hide configuration, edge cases, and secondary options.
5. **Fast feedback**: The user should feel like something happened the moment they hit scan.
